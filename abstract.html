ABSTRACT



Student performance prediction has become a critical area of research in educational data mining, aiming to improve academic outcomes and personalize learning experiences. This project seeks to develop a robust prediction model to evaluate students' academic performance based on various factors such as demographic information, socioeconomic background, past academic records, attendance, engagement in extracurricular activities, and behavioral patterns.

Leveraging machine learning algorithms, the project processes and analyzes large datasets to identify patterns and correlations influencing academic success. Techniques such as feature selection, classification, and regression are employed to build predictive models with high accuracy. Algorithms like Decision Trees, Random Forests, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and Neural Networks are explored to find the optimal approach for diverse datasets.

The projects objectives include providing educators and policymakers with actionable insights, enabling early identification of at-risk students, and promoting targeted interventions to enhance academic performance. By integrating explainable AI, the system ensures transparency in predictions, fostering trust and facilitating informed decision-making.

Ultimately, this project aims to contribute to the broader goal of equitable and quality education, empowering institutions to foster academic excellence and holistic development for all learners.
